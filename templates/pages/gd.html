Group Discussion




How To Takepart In A Group Discussion In An Interview?

1. Know about the current topics Usually, employers prefer asking current topics for a GD. To prepare in advance for the GD, consider reading a newspaper and magazine and watching television regularly to stay up-to-date with the current topics.
2. Practice before the group discussion To excel in your group discussion, participate in a mock GD and practice the situation before attending the interview process.
3. Practice time management As the entire discussion lasts for only 10-15 minutes, it is essential to practice time management.
4. Define the topic Making an assertive statement at the start can help you attract the employer's attention.
5. Listen to what others are saying Listening skills are essential in a group discussion because it helps understand the topic others are discussing and show respect for others' viewpoints. {% comment %}
6. Avoid deviating from the topic If someone is drifting away from the topic during a discussion, try to bring back the discussion on the topic. Straying away or deviating from the discussion is not acceptable.
7. Summarise the discussion After the moderator gestures to end the discussion, be the first one to summarise the GD and highlight the essential points. {% endcomment %}


Tips For Participating In Group Discussion

1. Initiate the discussion A group discussion allows you to lead the discussion. When the GD moderator announces a certain topic, try to grasp some ideas, write important points and start the discussion.
2. Avoid being aggressive When discussing a topic with a group, staying firm on your ideas is essential. If you have a conflict of interest with other candidates, avoid getting aggressive to prove your point to others.
3. Improve your thinking and communication Clearly conveying your thought process and viewpoints is essential for group discussion.
4. Showcase your teamwork Group discussion is a teamwork skill and represents how well you can connect with others in a team environment.
5. Show your confidence Confidence is the key to your success in a group discussion. Showing confidence in your abilities and knowledge of the subject can differentiate you from others.
6. Dress professionally While wearing the right clothes might be the last thing on your mind, dressing professionally can reflect your professionalism. It is essential to wear formal attire.
7. Be polite during the discussion Another thing to keep in mind while disagreeing with someone's viewpoint is speaking politely and presenting your opinion without raising your voice.
8. Avoid dominance during the discussion Leading a discussion is different from dominating it. As you are discussing a point, give a chance to other speakers.

Steps of Effective Group Discussion

A GD is a method used by organisations to analyse the skills of candidates and decide whether their personality traits are desirable for the job or not. While facing a GD, the following steps should be performed:

*Initiate
*Lead
*Summarise

Initiate

If you want to quickly grab the attention of assessors, then start the GD. However, you must have good knowledge or understanding of the subject being discussed.
To make your speech more interesting, you can start with a relevant quote or a short/interesting story; but keep track of time.

Lead

There might be a situation when you do not have enough knowledge to start a discussion. In that case, wait, watch and listen to others.
As soon as you get an opening, jump in and take charge. Move the conversation forward to make it impactful. However, remember not to over-drag the topic. Sometimes, less is more.

Summarise

Closing a GD is another opportunity to get the attention of the evaluators. Recap the discussion, connect the dots, highlight the key points and summarise them.
Make sure that the summary includes both the positive and negative viewpoints on the topic presented by the candidates.

Common GD Topics

1) Can we dream of hosting the Olympics?
2) EQ or IQ
3) Economic growth is more important than Ecological protection
4) Solution of corruption is a mirage till we catch top public figure
5) Non execution of GST bill might herald end of present government
6) Is the youth of India confident or confused?
7) Youth in Politics
8) Chinese Goods vs Indian Goods
9) Is compulsory attendance really needed in college?
10) Should national anthem be played in cinema halls?
